POEMS ABOUT THE CLOUD

>> a collection of verse for the distributed age
[ I. The Cloud ]
Free verse

They told you it was "the cloud."
Ethereal. Floating. Weightless.
A gentle mist of computation
raining data upon your grateful devices.

It is a warehouse in Virginia.
It is three million servers humming
in a building the size of a Costco,
cooled by the tears of on-call engineers
and a truly alarming amount of electricity.

Your photos live there now.
Next to a stranger's photos.
Next to a database of restaurant reviews.
Next to something the government won't talk about.
All in the same building. All on the same rack, possibly.

But sure. "The cloud."
Very poetic.
Very not-a-warehouse-in-Virginia.

[ II. Ode to AWS Billing ]
A collection of haiku

Left one instance on.
Went on vacation. Came back.
Four thousand dollars.

Free tier, they promised.
Twelve months of complimentary
existential dread.

CloudWatch alarm rings.
Budget exceeded again.
NAT Gateway. Why.

I read the pricing.
Per request, per gigabyte,
per will to live, gone.

[ III. Serverless ]
A limerick

A developer cried, "It's serverless!"
"No servers to patch, what a blessing!"
But under the hood,
Where nobody could look,
Were six thousand servers, just guessing.

Serverless: a word that means "we didn't eliminate servers, we eliminated your ability to find them when something breaks at 3am."

[ IV. My Docker Container ]
An elegy for the ephemeral

You were born from a Dockerfile,
alpine:3.18, lean and purposeful.
You knew only one thing: serve port 8080.
And you did it beautifully.

For eleven minutes.

Then Kubernetes decided you were unhealthy.
Your liveness probe returned 503. Once.
Just once. You were having a bad moment.
We all have bad moments.

But there is no forgiveness in orchestration.
Only SIGTERM. Then SIGKILL.
Your replacement was already running
before your logs finished flushing.
It didn't even know you existed.

Goodbye, pod/api-server-7f8b9c6d4-xk2mv.
You were a good container.
You deserved more than eleven minutes.

[ V. The Terraform Sonnet ]
Shakespearean sonnet (loosely)

Shall I compare thee to a terraform plan?
Thou art more lovely and more terrifying.
"847 resources to destroy" — oh man,
I only changed a tag. What are you modifying?

The state file locked by someone who went home,
The drift detected in a module I don't own,
The backend config pointing at a different zone,
The import block for things already known.

And yet I type "yes" when prompted in the night,
For what is infrastructure but a prayer?
A YAML dream, a JSON-laden fright,
A hope that DNS will propagate somewhere.

So long as clouds exist and servers hum,
So long lives terraform apply, and we are dumb.

[ VI. Microservices: A Requiem ]
Spoken word

We had a monolith. It worked.
But it wasn't fashionable.

So we split it. Into services.
Forty-seven services.
Each one doing the job
of a single function in the monolith
but now with its own database,
its own deploy pipeline,
its own on-call rotation,
and its own existential crisis
about whether it's a service or a library.

The latency went up.
The complexity went up.
The headcount went up.
The only thing that went down
was the system.
Frequently.
