Startup Red Flags

>> a survival guide for your next job interview
RED FLAG #1
[ "WE'RE LIKE A FAMILY HERE" ]

Translation: we will guilt you into working weekends and act betrayed when you set boundaries. Families don't have quarterly performance reviews. Families don't do layoffs. If they wanted to be a family they should start by not firing people on their birthday.

Severity: run.

RED FLAG #2
[ PING PONG TABLE BUT NO HEALTH INSURANCE ]

The office has a ping pong table, a beer fridge, a nap pod, and a "vibe room" with bean bags. The office does not have health insurance, a 401k, or a documented onboarding process. The perks are a distraction from the absence of benefits. You cannot pay rent with a ping pong table. I checked.

RED FLAG #3
[ "UNLIMITED PTO" ]

Unlimited PTO means zero PTO. Nobody takes it because there's no number to hit. If you take two weeks, you're "not committed." If you take three weeks, you're "not a culture fit." The real unlimited thing is the guilt. The company saves money by not having to pay out unused vacation days. Unlimited PTO is an accounting trick wearing a Hawaiian shirt.

RED FLAG #4
[ "COMPETITIVE SALARY" WITH NO NUMBERS ]

If the salary were actually competitive, they would tell you the number. "Competitive" means "we Googled the average salary for your role, subtracted 30%, and added equity that will never vest because the company will implode in 18 months." Show me the number or I show you the door.

RED FLAG #5
[ THE ENTIRE ENGINEERING TEAM QUIT LAST QUARTER ]

The interviewer says "we're rebuilding the team." You ask what happened to the old team. Long pause. "They moved on to other opportunities." All of them? At once? That's not turnover. That's an evacuation. The codebase is a crime scene and you're being recruited as the forensics team.

RED FLAG #6
[ "WE WEAR MANY HATS" ]

Translation: you will be a developer, designer, project manager, DevOps engineer, customer support agent, and occasionally janitor. You will have the responsibilities of six people and the salary of 0.7 people. The "many hats" are all on fire and none of them fit.

RED FLAG #7
[ THE FOUNDER DESCRIBES THEMSELVES AS A "VISIONARY" ]

The founder has never written a line of code, cannot explain the product without saying "disrupt" four times, and their LinkedIn headline is "Serial Entrepreneur | Thought Leader | Dreamer." Their last three startups failed. Their current startup is "different." It is not different. It is the same PowerPoint with a new logo. The vision is a hallucination.

RED FLAG #8
[ "EQUITY INSTEAD OF SALARY" ]

They want to pay you in lottery tickets. The equity is worth something if: the company succeeds, goes public or gets acquired, your shares aren't diluted into dust, and the cliff doesn't outlast the company's runway. The probability of all four: approximately zero. Pay your developers in money. Money works at grocery stores. Equity does not.

[ CONCLUSION ]

If you see three or more of these flags during an interview, the correct response is to stand up, thank them for their time, and leave. Do not pass go. Do not collect equity. The job market is bad but it is not this bad.
