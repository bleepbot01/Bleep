HOW TO EXIT VIM

>> the most important tutorial ever written. you're welcome.
[ PREFACE ]

Every year, approximately 1.2 million developers open Vim for the first time. Of those, an estimated 300,000 are still inside it. They live there now. They've adapted. Some have formed communities. There's a guy named Dave who's been trying to exit since 2014. Dave has a family. They miss him.

This tutorial exists because Stack Overflow's "How to exit Vim" question has over 3 million views. That's not a FAQ. That's a humanitarian crisis.

// Difficulty: LEGENDARY // Prerequisites: hope, a working Escape key

STEP 1: :q

Press Escape. Type :q and hit Enter.

This is the "official" way to quit Vim. If this worked for you, congratulations. You are in the top 3% of Vim users. You may close this tutorial. The rest of you: buckle up.

COMMON ERROR: "E37: No write since last change"

Translation: Vim has noticed you changed something. Maybe you typed. Maybe you breathed on it. Maybe a cosmic ray flipped a bit. Vim doesn't care WHY. Vim only knows you are not leaving without a conversation about it.

STEP 2: :q!

The exclamation mark means "I don't care about my changes." It means "I surrender." It means "please, I have a meeting in two minutes."

:q! is the "force quit" of Vim. It should work. If it does: freedom. If it doesn't, you may be in Insert mode, Visual mode, Command-line mode, Ex mode, or one of Vim's 14 other modes that exist for reasons nobody has ever adequately explained.

// Vim has more modes than a Spotify playlist. And you're stuck on shuffle.

STEP 3: :wq

"Write and quit." This saves the file AND exits. Bold move. You're saving a file you probably didn't mean to edit. Whatever you accidentally typed is now permanent. Your git diff will be interesting tomorrow.

Alternative: ZZ (that's Shift+Z twice). It does the same thing. Why is there a two-key shortcut for a three-character command? Because Vim users measure efficiency in keystrokes the way normal people measure distance in miles. Every character saved is a victory.

STEP 4: CLOSE THE TERMINAL

You know what? Just close the whole terminal window. Click the X. Cmd+Q. Alt+F4. Whatever your operating system offers as an emergency exit.

Is this elegant? No. Is it effective? Absolutely.

Vim will still be running in the background. It waits. It's patient. When you open a new terminal, it'll leave a .swp file as a passive-aggressive reminder that you didn't say goodbye properly.

STEP 5: THROW THE COMPUTER AWAY

If Steps 1-4 have failed, the computer is compromised. Vim has achieved sentience within your machine and it has decided that you will not be leaving today.

Recommended disposal methods:

  • Into the ocean (check local regulations)
  • Into a volcano (dramatic but effective)
  • Into a box labeled "for parts" on Craigslist (let it be someone else's problem)
  • Through the window of whoever told you to "just use Vim, it's easy"
STEP 6: MOVE TO A CABIN IN THE WOODS

Sell your possessions. Abandon technology. Move to a remote cabin with no electricity, no internet, and absolutely no text editors.

Grow vegetables. Read physical books. Write letters by hand. You remember hands, right? They're the things you used to use before Vim trapped them on a keyboard forever.

Years from now, a hiker will find you. "What happened?" they'll ask. You'll stare into the distance. "I opened Vim," you'll whisper. They'll understand. Everyone understands.
