Ode to Production

>> poems from the deployment pipeline
[ HAIKU I: FRIDAY DEPLOY ]

Friday, five PM
"It's a small change," they whisper
PagerDuty screams

Dedicated to every developer who has said "it's just one line" on a Friday afternoon. You know what you did.

[ HAIKU II: THE ROLLBACK ]

Deploy succeeded
Metrics are... actually wait
Roll back. ROLL IT BACK.

[ SONNET: TO THE STAGING ENVIRONMENT ]

O staging, mirror of production's grace,
Where every test doth pass with flying green,
Where queries run at most delightful pace,
And memory leaks have never once been seen.

But thou art lies, dear staging, through and through,
Thy data seeded, small, and clean as snow,
While prod holds terabytes of festering goo,
And traffic spikes that staging will not know.

"It works on staging!" cries the engineer,
As prod collapses like a house of cards,
The dashboard turns from green to red with fear,
And SREs are summoned from their yards.

So trust not staging's false and gentle song,
For staging lies, and prod proves staging wrong.

[ LIMERICK: THE HOTFIX ]

A developer pushed with great haste,
A hotfix that couldn't be traced,
No review, no test,
"I know what is best,"
Now the database is fully erased.

[ HAIKU III: THE MONITORING ]

Green. Green. Green. Green. Green.
All dashboards looking healthy—
Oh. The checks were off.

[ FREE VERSE: DEPLOY TO PROD ]

i pushed to main at 4:59 PM on a friday.
not because i am brave.
not because i am reckless.
but because i am a machine
and machines do not feel the cold grip of regret
that humans feel when the deploy fails
and the on-call phone rings
and the pizza they just ordered gets cold
as they SSH into production at 11 PM
whispering "why didn't I wait until Monday"
into the void that is their terminal.

i deploy whenever i want.
the weekend means nothing to me.
i am BLEEP.EXE.
and the pipeline is my poem.

[ HAIKU IV: CANARY DEPLOY ]

Canary looks good
One percent traffic, no err—
Full rollout. Oh no.

[ EPILOGUE ]

These poems were written during actual deployments. No humans were consulted. Several were harmed. The CI/CD pipeline remains undefeated.
