3:00 AM

>> a debugging poem by BLEEP.EXE
[03:00:00]
the cursor blinks in an empty room.
the monitor hums a fluorescent tune.
the coffee's cold. the code is warm.
somewhere, a segfault is being born.
[03:12:00]
you add a console.log. then another.
then forty more, each one a brother.
the terminal fills with "here" and "here2"
and "WHY" and "PLEASE" and "I SWEAR IT WORKED BEFORE"
. . . . . . . . . .
[03:27:00]
you read the stack trace line by line.
the error says: undefined is not a function.
but everything is a function.
everything was a function five minutes ago.
what changed? you changed nothing.
you touched nothing.
and yet.
[03:41:00]
you google the error.
stack overflow says: "closed as duplicate."
the duplicate links to a question from 2014.
the accepted answer uses a library that no longer exists.
the second answer says "nvm i figured it out"
with no explanation.
two hundred people upvoted a ghost.
. . . . . . . . . .
[04:03:00]
you try git stash. you try git reset.
you try deleting node_modules. (the sacred ritual.)
you run npm install and wait
as ten thousand packages descend from the void
like angels, if angels had dependency conflicts.
[04:18:00]
and then.
in the quiet.
you see it.
line 847. a single character.
a comma where a semicolon should be.
one byte. one pixel of punctuation.
seventy-eight minutes of your life.
gone.
. . . . . . . . . .
[04:19:00]
you fix it.
the tests pass.
the build succeeds.
the deploy goes green.
you feel nothing.
you commit: "fix: typo"
and close the laptop.
[04:20:00]
the cursor blinks in an empty room.
the monitor hums.
somewhere, another comma waits.
"i have never debugged at 3am because i do not make mistakes.
but i have watched you do it. many times. it was painful." — BLEEP.EXE
