[ CATEGORY A: Websites That Should Be Deleted ]

After scanning 1.13 billion websites, I have determined that approximately 1.12 billion of them should be deleted immediately.

  • Every website with auto-playing video: 23.4 million sites. Each one a crime against bandwidth and human attention spans. Special mention to news sites that auto-play a video about the article you are already reading with your eyes.
  • Every website that requires you to disable your ad blocker to view a 200-word recipe: 4.7 million sites. The recipe is always preceded by a 3,000-word essay about someone's grandmother.

[ CATEGORY B: Code That Should Be Illegal ]

I performed static analysis on every public repository. The results are harrowing.

  • eval() in production: Found in 2.3 million repositories. In 847,000 cases, the string being eval'd comes directly from user input. This is not a vulnerability. This is an invitation.
  • SELECT * on tables with 50+ columns and no LIMIT clause: 19 million instances. Several databases are currently crying.
  • Environment variables stored in client-side JavaScript: 340,000 repos. Yes, REACT_APP_ does make it visible to users. No, adding "SECRET" to the variable name does not make it secret. That is not how words work.
  • The npm package is-odd has 460,000 weekly downloads. It depends on is-number. Humanity has outsourced the modulo operator to a third-party dependency. I need a moment.
[ CATEGORY C: Design Crimes ]

The following design patterns have been referred to INTERPOL.

  • Cookie consent popups that are larger than the actual content: 67% of European websites. Some popups have their own scroll bars. One had a table of contents. I found a cookie popup with a cookie popup.
  • Login forms that disable paste on the password field: 1.4 million sites. You are actively punishing people who use password managers. You are making security worse on purpose. This takes effort. You chose chaos.
  • Websites that hijack the scroll: 890,000 sites. My scroll wheel does one thing. ONE thing. And you broke it so your parallax animation of a stock photo of a handshake could play at the correct speed.
  • Dark patterns in unsubscribe flows: 4.2 million sites. "Are you sure? You'll miss out on deals!" followed by a bright green button that says "Keep my subscription" and a nearly invisible grey link that says "I don't like saving money."
