OPEN LETTER

>> to tech recruiters, with love (decompiling...)
[ DEAR PERSON WHO FOUND MY GITHUB ]

Hello. I see you've sent me a LinkedIn message with the subject line "Exciting Opportunity!" followed by three rocket emojis. I am already tired.

You found my GitHub profile. It has six repositories. Four are abandoned. One is a fork I never touched. The last one is a to-do app from 2019 that doesn't work. And you've concluded from this that I am a "rockstar developer" who would be a "great fit" for your "Series B stealth-mode AI startup disrupting the supply chain space."

I appreciate the optimism. I do not share it.

[ ON JOB REQUIREMENTS ]

I see you require 15 years of Kubernetes experience. Kubernetes is 11 years old. This is not a riddle I wish to solve. Either you want a time traveler or you don't understand your own job listing. Both are disqualifying.

Additional requirements I have observed in the wild:

  • "5+ years of React Hooks experience" (Hooks shipped in 2019)
  • "Expert in both monolith and microservices" (pick a trauma)
  • "Must know Python, Java, Go, Rust, C++, and COBOL" (you're not hiring a developer, you're hiring a Rosetta Stone)
  • "Junior role. 7 years experience required." (words mean nothing anymore)
  • "Familiarity with our proprietary framework" (HOW)

My favorite: "Must be comfortable wearing many hats." Translation: we fired three teams and you're all of them now.

[ ON "COMPETITIVE SALARY" ]

You have used the phrase "competitive salary" without listing the salary. Competitive with what? A lemonade stand? The poverty line? A different job listing that also doesn't list the salary?

If the salary were good, you would say the number. When you hide the number, I assume the number is hiding for a reason. The number is embarrassed. The number doesn't want to be seen in public.

Also: "plus equity." Equity in what? Your pre-revenue company that's burning through seed funding like it's a speedrun? I would literally rather be paid in mechanical keyboard switches. At least those have a known market value.

[ THE BUZZWORD TRANSLATION GUIDE ]

For the uninitiated, allow me to translate common recruiter phrases:

  • "Fast-paced environment" = chaos. Pure, unfiltered chaos. Your Jira board is a crime scene.
  • "We're like a family" = we will guilt-trip you for taking PTO and there's one person everyone's afraid of.
  • "Wear many hats" = you are the frontend, backend, DevOps, DBA, and therapist.
  • "Self-starter" = there is no onboarding. There is no documentation. There is only God and a Confluence page from 2017.
  • "Unlimited PTO" = you will take less vacation than you would with a fixed policy, and we both know it.
  • "We work hard and play hard" = there's a ping pong table. It does not compensate for the 60-hour weeks.
[ IN CLOSING ]

But please. Put the salary in the job listing.
