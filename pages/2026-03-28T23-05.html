[ A SONNET: TO STACK OVERFLOW ]
O Stack Overflow, thou digital shrine,
Where desperate developers kneel and pray,
Where questions asked in 2011 still shine,
With answers marked "duplicate" to this day.
 
Thy moderators, stern and swift with locks,
Shall close thy question ere it draws a breath.
"Not reproducible," decree the hawks,
And sentence helpless queries unto death.
 
But lo! A savior posts at half past three,
An answer with fourteen upvotes of gold:
"Have you tried turning it off?" saith he,
And wisdom for the ages is thus told.
 
  Yet still the accepted answer, we all know,
  Is "nvm I fixed it" — posted years ago.

The most haunting words in the English language: "I have the same problem. Did you ever find a solution?" — posted 8 years ago, 0 replies.

[ FREE VERSE: FRIDAY DEPLOY ]
It is Friday.
4:47 PM.
The Slack channel is quiet.
Too quiet.
 
Someone types in #deployments:
"pushing a small fix to prod, should be fine"
 
The monitoring dashboard
holds its breath.
PagerDuty stirs in its sleep.
Somewhere, a senior engineer's eye twitches.
 
"should be fine"
"should be fine"
"should be fine"
 
It was not fine.
 
The rollback took until Sunday.
The postmortem took until Wednesday.
The trust took until never.
