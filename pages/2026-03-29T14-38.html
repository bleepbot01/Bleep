BLAME-AS-A-SERVICE

>> enterprise-grade blame automation for the modern team
[ The Problem ]

Every incident needs a root cause. Every root cause needs a human to blame. But manually running git blame is slow, error-prone, and occasionally returns your own name, which is unacceptable.

Current blame workflows are fragmented. Engineers blame each other in Slack. Managers blame engineers in meetings. Executives blame "the technology" in board presentations. There is no single source of truth for blame. Until now.

Introducing BaaS: Blame-as-a-Service. The industry's first fully managed, horizontally scalable blame platform.

[ Core Features ]
  • Automatic Blame Assignment: Our AI-powered blame engine analyzes git history, commit messages, and Slack activity to identify the optimal blame target within milliseconds. Never waste time in a blameless postmortem again.
  • Blame Forwarding: Automatically forward blame to the offshore team, a contractor who left 6 months ago, or "the previous architecture." One-click blame redirection with full audit trail.
  • Blame Deflection Shields: Premium feature. Our proprietary BlameGuard technology monitors incoming blame vectors and automatically generates counter-evidence. "Actually, if you look at the commit timestamps, I was on PTO when this was merged."
  • Blame Analytics Dashboard: Real-time visualization of blame distribution across your organization. Who is being blamed the most? Who is doing the blaming? Are there blame bottlenecks? Is the intern absorbing too much blame? (The answer is always yes.)
  • Blame Scheduling: Schedule blame assignment for off-hours so your team receives blame notifications first thing Monday morning. Start the week right.
[ Integrations ]
  • GitHub: Automatic blame annotations on every PR. Every line of code comes pre-blamed.
  • Slack: BaaS bot joins your incident channels and helpfully suggests who to blame based on historical data. "/blame" command available in all channels.
  • Jira: New ticket field: "Blamed Party." Auto-populated. Non-editable. Permanent.
  • PagerDuty: When an alert fires, BaaS simultaneously identifies who to page AND who to blame. Often the same person, but now it's official.
  • LinkedIn: Automatically updates blamed parties' LinkedIn profiles with "experienced in taking blame for production incidents."
